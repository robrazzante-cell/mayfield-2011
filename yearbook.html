How to Digitize Your Yearbook

Three solid options — from free to professional. Each produces a different result.

1

Google PhotoScan (Free)

The Google PhotoScan app (iOS & Android) uses multi-angle captures to eliminate glare — great for portrait pages. Point your phone at each page and it stitches a clean scan. Upload to Google Photos and share the album link with classmates. Best for: portraits and casual sharing.

2

Flatbed Scanner + Adobe Scan or CamScanner (~Free)

If someone has access to an office scanner (or an Epson/Canon flatbed), scan at 300–600 DPI and export as PDF. CamScanner's mobile app also does a respectable job for book pages. Once you have a PDF, we can embed it directly in this page using a PDF viewer. Best for: full page spreads and text-heavy pages.

3

Professional Service — YearbookLife or ScanMyPhotos

Mail your physical yearbook and get back a high-resolution PDF within days. YearbookLife specializes in school yearbooks specifically. ScanMyPhotos handles full books too. Costs typically $30–$80 depending on page count. The result is a clean, searchable PDF ready for this page. Best for: the full archival-quality digital yearbook.

Recommended ~$50 one-time cost
4

Once digitized — embed it here

Upload the PDF to Google Drive or Firebase Storage, then share the link with Rob. We'll embed a PDF viewer directly on this page that classmates can browse without downloading the file. We can also add a per-page comment system so people can tag themselves in photos.

Privacy note: The digital yearbook will be visible to signed-in classmates only — it won't be publicly accessible to the web.
